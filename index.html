Biography

Dr. Ye Jiqing, doctor of philosophy, is currently an Associate Researcher and Distinguished Professor at Anhui Medical University. His research focuses on significant targets in antimicrobial and antitumor areas. He employs virtual screening and rational design methods to identify new lead compounds and continues to investigate structure-activity relationships to discover novel candidate compounds with innovative structures.

Research Area
Computational Medicinal Chemistry
Our focus lies in the development and application of innovative computational and theoretical methods to offer new physical-chemical insights into critical biological processes. Additionally, we aim to enable the rational design of modulators to investigate key cellular pathways and to advance their potential therapeutic applications. Computationl methods such as molecular modeling, virtual screening, and quantitative structure-activity relationship (QSAR) analysis, are used to predict the interactions between drugs and biological targets, optimize drug properties, and identify potential side effects. Briefly, this interdisciplinary approach that integrates principles from chemistry, biology, and computer science to are used to accelerate drug discovery and improve the efficacy and safety of therapeutic agents.
Synthetic Medicinal Chemistry
Synthetic medicinal chemistry constitutes a significant area of our research. Our primary focus is the development of novel chemical entities designed to target protein-protein interactions for potential applications as antimicrobial and anticancer agents. This involves a computational approach for molecular design, followed by synthesis and analytical characterization. We also emphasize the optimization of drug-like properties, such as lipophilicity, solubility, and stability. Newly synthesized compounds undergo enzymatic assays and subsequent in vitro testing to evaluate their efficacy and potential therapeutic value.
